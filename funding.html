Request Funding

Funding Request Form

Who Can Apply

All 8CSC committees, Programming Board (PB), and external organizations.

When to Apply

  • At least 1 month before your event.
  • Fall Quarter events may need requests submitted the prior Spring.
  • Late requests may be denied if there isn’t enough time for review.

What to Submit

  • Completed Funding Request Form.
  • Itemized Budget (using provided template) including:
    • Description, cost, vendor, and justification.
    • Vendors must follow the Ethical Spending Guidelines (ESG).
    • Avoid Divestment List, prioritize Investment List.

Review Process

  • Submission: Submit by Friday at 10:00AM PST.
  • Initial Review: Finance reviews Fridays at 11:00AM PST.
  • Interview (if needed): Finance may request a meeting for clarification.
  • Final Decision: 8CSC votes for approval.
    • Standing events: Only budget requires vote.
    • New events: Proposal + budget require vote.
  • Notification: Funding decision emailed to requester.
Submit Request

Post-Funding Form

What’s Required

  • Expense Documentation: Itemized receipts for all purchases.
  • Impact Metrics: Outcomes and impact explanation (no fabricated metrics).
  • Ethical Spending Verification: Explanation of how spending aligned with ESG.

Review

The Finance committee will verify that all purchases follow approved vendors and guidelines. Violations will be reported by email to the organization.

Eligibility Requirements

  • Applicants must be an 8CSC committee, Programming Board (PB), or an external organization collaborating with Eighth College.
  • Requests must be submitted at least 1 month before the event. Fall Quarter events may require submission in the prior Spring.
View Budget Procedures

Ethical Spending Guidelines (ESG)

Community Investment Requirement

At least 60% of total annual spending and individual event spending must support local/small businesses, Black-owned businesses, Refugee-owned businesses, Sustainable businesses, and Mutual aid efforts or local initiatives.

The remaining up to 40% may be used flexibly for programmatic needs, provided all spending still complies with ESG.

Living Document

  • ESG is reviewed quarterly, with updates based on student input, vendor performance, and local economic context.
  • Finance/CPP committees review feedback weekly and present updates at GBM.
  • Changes to the Investment/Divestment Lists require a simple majority vote by Council.
  • Emergency updates may be voted electronically via Slack.

Programming Board (PB) Flexibility Clause

  • PB may request limited-use exceptions from the Divestment List in emergencies.
  • Exceptions must be communicated to Finance and documented in the Post-Funding Form.
  • Limited to one exception per quarter, unless extraordinary circumstances apply.
  • Exceptions cannot be used to bypass the 60% Community Investment Requirement.
